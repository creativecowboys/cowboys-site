1. Strategy
This is the brief behind the three landing pages. It's intentionally short — the heavy lifting is in the audit spreadsheet ("Franklin TN — Competitor Audit.xlsx") and the page-by-page outlines that follow.
1.1  The shape of the Franklin TN market
Across 22 audited landing pages spanning the three services, page-1 of the Franklin TN SERPs is dominated by one operator wearing four hats. JLB Works, Horton Group, CFM Digital, and Nice Branding all share the same Franklin office at 200 Seaboard Lane, share template assets, share a CDN (thewebserviceteam.com), and use near-identical copy patterns. They occupy 3–4 of every top 10 listing for each service category.
Everyone else fills the remaining slots: Muletown Digital (Columbia HQ, strongest content + pricing transparency), Franklin Web Design (exact-match domain, thin trust signals), Zach Sean and Virtue Design (boutique solo studios), Marketing Hatchery (Vanderbilt logo, broken stats), WebJi (hyper-local SEO, zero proof), Google Ads Agency Nashville (transparent flat pricing, no local office), Atomic Social (Scottsdale AZ pretending to be Franklin).
The takeaway: one differentiated, locally-grounded challenger with real proof can leapfrog the entire field. The cluster's identical templates mean their badges have stopped doing the work badges are supposed to do — they look like substitutes for real proof, not amplifiers of it.
1.2  Creative Cowboys' positioning angle
Important context for the entire pack: Creative Cowboys is primarily a Southeast agency expanding into Franklin and Williamson County. We do not claim Franklin tenure. We do lead with the credentials we actually have — total revenue generated for clients, total #1 rankings achieved, total sites built, years in business — and let those carry the trust load that Franklin-specific case studies would have carried.
The Franklin market has two archetypes and a gap between them:
	•	Archetype A — Faceless agencies with badge stacks and 20-year claims. JLB, Horton, CFM, Nice Branding. They look corporate and credible but generic, dated, and proof-thin.
	•	Archetype B — Solo boutique studios with personality and small portfolios. Zach Sean, Virtue Design. They feel human but lack scale and outcome data.
Position Creative Cowboys in the gap: a senior Southeast team bringing real portfolio-scale credentials (millions in client revenue, hundreds of #1 rankings, dozens of named case studies) to a Franklin market where the incumbents lead with vague badges and the boutiques lead with personality but no scale. The CC brand orange + lime/lavender/coral palette already separates us visually from the Seaboard cluster's tired blue/grey templates.
Stance on tenure. We don't volunteer that we're new to Franklin. We don't claim we're local. We let the agency-wide proof carry the page and let the buyer assume what they assume. Anything we say about Williamson County is forward-tense ("the neighborhoods we'll rank you in," "the verticals we serve here") rather than past-tense ("businesses we've built sites for in Cool Springs").
Tagline candidates
	•	Senior Southeast agency. Now serving Franklin.
	•	The work Williamson County has been waiting for.
	•	The agency businesses actually keep.
Lock in one and use it consistently across all three landing pages. None of these claim local tenure; all of them frame the buyer benefit.
1.3  Differentiators to lead with on every page
Cross-referenced against what the field uniformly fails to do. Each is a moat that turns out to be wide open.
	•	Real named case studies from the Southeast portfolio, with before/after numbers and screenshots. The work is what it is — verticals overlap with Franklin demand, the numbers are real, and we don't pretend the projects happened in Cool Springs. Nobody on page 1 has named inline case studies regardless.
	•	Aggregate proof at scale: $2M+ in revenue generated for clients, 500+ #1 organic rankings achieved, 100+ websites built. Numbers that beat any local agency's quiet portfolio.
	•	Transparent 3-tier pricing on the page itself. Only Muletown and Google Ads Agency Nashville publish pricing. Everyone else is opaque.
	•	Hyper-local copy that names Cool Springs, Westhaven, Berry Farms, McKay's Mill, Downtown Franklin, The Factory, Carothers Pkwy — framed as the neighborhoods we'll serve, not the neighborhoods we've been in. Only WebJi gets close on local specificity, and WebJi has zero real proof.
	•	Real founder + team faces. The Seaboard cluster brands are faceless. Putting humans on the page is differentiator-grade.
	•	2026-quality visual design that proves the service. JLB still serves an 'Update your browser' popup. Sub-1.5s page load. Display Lighthouse score in the hero.
	•	Live Google reviews feed embedded. Only CFM hints at this. Nobody embeds it live.
	•	Multi-channel paid media positioning (Google + Meta + LinkedIn + YouTube), not Google-Ads-only. Wide-open lane.
	•	Free 48-hour productized diagnostic per service (Franklin Web Design Scorecard, Franklin SEO Audit, Franklin Google Ads Audit). Lead magnet + qualifier.
	•	Risk reversal: '30-day rolling, cancel anytime. You own all accounts, data, and creative.' Only Google Ads Agency Nashville says this.
1.4  Voice and tone
The Seaboard cluster's voice is 'corporate marketing brochure 2014' — superlatives, vague ROI claims, '#1 voted best' without sources. We win by sounding like a real person who knows the work and the town.
	•	Concrete over abstract. 'Cool Springs HVAC client: $84 → $19 CPL in 60 days' beats 'we improve ROI.'
	•	Confident, not breathless. Skip 'world-class', 'cutting-edge', 'innovative'.
	•	Local voice without overdoing the Southern thing. Virtue Design's 'Southern charm' is sweet but soft. We're not playing the warm-blanket angle.
	•	Senior-designer candor. We tell a Franklin restaurant owner their Squarespace site is fine and they don't need us yet. That posture is impossible to fake and rare in the field.
